section_d::real_world_applications

Where these building blocks actually ship.

Five systems people use every day, and which Week 1 concepts make each one work.

app::search

Search Engines

Concepts used: Tokenization, Stop-word Removal, Stemming/Lemmatization, TF-IDF, Word Embeddings.

Why needed: Queries and documents must be normalized to the same form before they can be matched, and terms need weighting so rare, meaningful words outrank common ones.

Expected benefit: Faster, more relevant results even when the query wording doesn't exactly match the document.

app::chatbots

Chatbots & Virtual Assistants

Concepts used: Tokenization, Lemmatization, Contextual Embeddings, Transformer-based Representations.

Why needed: Understanding user intent requires context-aware meaning, not just keyword matching, since the same words can mean different things.

Expected benefit: More natural, context-aware conversations and accurate intent detection.

app::sentiment

Sentiment Analysis

Concepts used: Noise Removal, Tokenization, Bag of Words / TF-IDF, Word Embeddings.

Why needed: Raw social media text is noisy; features need to capture emotional tone-bearing words, not just frequency.

Expected benefit: Businesses can gauge public opinion on products or events at scale, in near real time.

app::translation

Machine Translation

Concepts used: Sentence Segmentation, Tokenization, Word Embeddings, Transformer-based Representations.

Why needed: Sentences must be understood in full context to preserve meaning across languages with different grammar and word order.

Expected benefit: Fluent, context-correct translations rather than literal word-for-word substitutions.

app::summarization

Text Summarization

Concepts used: Sentence Segmentation, TF-IDF, Contextual Embeddings, Neural/Transformer Language Models.

Why needed: The system must judge which sentences or phrases carry the most important information in a document.

Expected benefit: Saves reading time while preserving the core meaning of long documents, news, or reports.
