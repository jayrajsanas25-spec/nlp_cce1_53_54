Every library, paper, and organization referenced across this repository, listed here with a short note on what it contributed and a link to the primary source.

Libraries & Frameworks

NLTK — Natural Language Toolkit

A foundational open-source Python library for classical NLP: tokenization, stemming, POS tagging, and corpus access. Used throughout this repository as the reference implementation for Porter stemming and stop-word lists.

nltk.org

spaCy

An industrial-strength, production-ready NLP library providing fast tokenization, sentence segmentation, lemmatization, and pretrained pipelines. Referenced in Section E as the open-source framework of choice.

spacy.io

Hugging Face

A hub and library ecosystem (Transformers, Datasets, Tokenizers) that hosts and distributes pretrained models like BERT and GPT, and standardizes subword tokenization schemes (BPE, WordPiece) referenced in the Tokenization and Contextual Embeddings cards.

huggingface.co

Research Institutions & Organizations

Stanford NLP Group

A leading academic research group producing foundational work in parsing, word vectors (GloVe), and core NLP theory that underlies much of Section A and B.

nlp.stanford.edu

Google Research

Origin of Word2Vec and BERT, two of the most influential developments referenced in this repository's Language Representation and Language Models sections.

research.google

OpenAI

Developer of the GPT family of transformer-based language models, referenced in Section E under recent research and industrial applications (ChatGPT).

openai.com/research

Core Textbook

Jurafsky, D. & Martin, J. H. — Speech and Language Processing

The standard academic textbook covering the theory behind tokenization, language modeling, and vector semantics. Used as the conceptual foundation for the definitions and working-principle explanations across Section A.

web.stanford.edu/~jurafsky/slp3

Citation format

References are listed in an informal author/organization — title — link format, suitable for an academic knowledge-artifact submission. Where a concept card or comparison draws directly on one of these sources, that connection is noted in the card or table itself.
