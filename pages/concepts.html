Tokenization

Turning continuous text into units a model can process.

Definition
Tokenization splits text into smaller units called tokens, such as words, subwords, or characters.
Purpose
It gives NLP systems discrete units that can be counted, encoded, or embedded.
Working Principle
A tokenizer applies language rules or learned subword patterns to identify valid boundaries.
Example
"NLP is useful" becomes ["NLP", "is", "useful"].
Advantages
Simple, foundational, and compatible with every later NLP stage.
Limitations
Contractions, punctuation, and languages without spaces can create ambiguous boundaries.
Applications
Search indexing, text classification, translation, chatbots, and language-model input.

TF-IDF

Highlighting words that are frequent locally but uncommon globally.

Definition
TF-IDF is a numerical score that combines term frequency with inverse document frequency.
Purpose
It identifies words that are important to one document but not common across the corpus.
Working Principle
The term's local frequency is multiplied by a logarithmic measure of its global rarity.
Example
"NLP" may receive a high score in an NLP article, while "the" receives a low score.
Advantages
Interpretable, efficient, and effective for keyword-based document features.
Limitations
It ignores word order, context, and semantic similarity and produces sparse vectors.
Applications
Search ranking, keyword extraction, document similarity, and classical text classification.

Lemmatization

Reducing inflected words to valid dictionary forms.

Definition
Lemmatization reduces a word to its meaningful dictionary base form, called a lemma.
Purpose
It groups grammatical variations while preserving a valid and readable word.
Working Principle
A lexical dictionary and part-of-speech information map each inflected form to its lemma.
Example
"studies" and "studying" become "study"; "better" can become "good".
Advantages
Produces linguistically accurate output and preserves meaning better than stemming.
Limitations
Requires language resources and grammatical analysis, making it slower than stemming.
Applications
Search, question answering, chatbots, topic analysis, and text normalization.

Word2Vec

Learning dense vectors from the company words keep.

Definition
Word2Vec is a neural method that learns dense vector representations of words.
Purpose
It captures semantic and syntactic relationships that count-based representations miss.
Working Principle
CBOW predicts a word from context, while Skip-gram predicts surrounding words from a target.
Example
Words such as "king", "queen", and "royal" appear close together in vector space.
Advantages
Compact vectors, efficient training, and useful semantic similarity relationships.
Limitations
Unseen words have no vector, and each word has one representation regardless of context.
Applications
Semantic search, recommendations, clustering, similarity analysis, and model features.
